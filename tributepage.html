Jagadish Chandra Bose

(1858-1937)

The Father of Modern Science in India

"The true worth of a scientist lies not in the accolades received, but in the impact made on society."

Biography


Jagadish Chandra Bose, born on November 30, 1858, in Mymensingh (now in Bangladesh), was a renowned Indian scientist, physicist, biologist, botanist, and archaeologist. He is known for his significant contributions to the fields of radio and microwave sciences, as well as plant biology. Bose conducted pioneering research on the response of plants to various stimuli, demonstrating that plants have a sensitivity and responses similar to animals. His work on wireless telegraphy laid the foundation for modern communication technology.

Bose was the first person to demonstrate the transmission of electromagnetic waves, and he subsequently invented various devices based on his research, including the coherer, an early form of radio detector. He made important contributions to the development of wireless communication, paving the way for the invention of radio by Guglielmo Marconi. Bose's research also encompassed the fields of biophysics and archaeology.

Throughout his life, Jagadish Chandra Bose remained committed to promoting scientific education in India and inspiring future generations of scientists. He strongly believed that science should be used for the betterment of society and emphasized the need for scientific research in solving societal problems.
